# OCNJ.ETH Pennsylvania Tax and Recordkeeping Checklist

This is a practical setup checklist, not individualized legal or tax advice.

## Before accepting meaningful revenue

### Choose an operating structure

Common starting options:

#### Sole proprietor using a fictitious name

Simplest administration.

Pennsylvania requires registration of a fictitious name when an individual conducts business under a name that does not identify the owner. A fictitious-name filing does not create liability protection.

#### Single-member Pennsylvania LLC

Often the cleaner choice when the project will:

- Sign advertising agreements
- Accept recurring payments
- Publish business claims
- Hire contractors
- Hold meaningful revenue
- Operate under a distinct public brand

An LLC can provide liability separation when properly formed and operated. A single-member LLC is commonly treated as a disregarded entity for federal tax purposes unless another tax election is made.

### Practical recommendation

For a serious commercial launch, discuss forming a single-member Pennsylvania LLC with an attorney or CPA before accepting larger sponsorships.

Possible legal entity name:

```text
OCNJ Media LLC
```

The website can still publicly use:

```text
OCNJ.ETH
```

Confirm name availability and trademark concerns before filing.

## Obtain an EIN

After forming the entity, obtain an EIN directly from the IRS at no cost.

Do not pay a third-party website for an EIN.

## Separate business finances

Use:

- A separate business checking account
- A dedicated business credit card
- A dedicated Stripe account
- A dedicated bookkeeping file
- A separate OCNJ.ETH wallet or clearly separated wallet records

Do not mix personal and business spending when it can be avoided.

## Record every payment

Record:

- Date received
- Customer
- Package
- Gross amount
- Processing fee
- Refunds
- Net deposit
- Contract term
- Invoice or receipt
- Start and end dates
- Business expenses connected to the sale

Income is reportable even when no tax form is issued.

## Crypto payments

Payment in cryptocurrency does not avoid tax.

When digital assets are received for services:

- Record the U.S.-dollar fair market value at the time received.
- Treat that value as business/self-employment income when applicable.
- That recorded value generally becomes the tax basis in the digital asset.
- A later sale or exchange can create a separate capital gain or loss.
- Save the transaction hash, timestamp, asset, amount, network and USD value.

Use a reliable crypto tax ledger or accounting process.

## Federal tax

A sole proprietor or disregarded single-member LLC generally reports business income and expenses on Schedule C.

Self-employed individuals can owe:

- Federal income tax
- Self-employment tax
- Estimated-tax underpayment penalties when payments are insufficient

Federal estimated-tax due dates are generally:

- April 15
- June 15
- September 15
- January 15

Because the owner may also have W-2 income, another option is increasing paycheck withholding instead of making separate estimated payments. A CPA can calculate the cleaner approach.

## Temporary tax reserve

Until a CPA calculates the actual amount, consider transferring approximately 30% to 35% of net business profit into a separate tax savings account.

This is a conservative planning reserve, not a statement of the actual tax rate.

Do not calculate the reserve only from the Stripe deposit. Use profit after legitimate business expenses, while keeping enough cash for operations.

## Pennsylvania and local taxes

Pennsylvania taxes net income from a commercial business.

Also review:

- Pennsylvania personal income tax or entity filings
- Local earned-income or net-profits tax
- Any business privilege or local registration requirements
- Pennsylvania sales-tax treatment of the exact advertising, digital-product or service package

Pennsylvania sales tax applies to tangible property, digital products and certain enumerated services. The treatment of an advertising/sponsorship package can depend on exactly what is sold. Confirm this with a Pennsylvania tax professional before deciding whether to collect sales tax.

## Deductible business expenses

Ordinary and necessary business expenses may include:

- Domain and hosting
- GitHub or software costs
- Design and development
- Business insurance
- Professional photography
- Advertising and marketing
- Payment-processing fees
- Accounting and legal fees
- Contractor payments
- Office supplies
- Qualified business-use portion of equipment or services
- Travel or mileage that meets business rules
- Tax-preparation costs relating to the business

Personal expenses are not converted into deductions merely because the website exists. Mixed expenses require proper allocation and documentation.

## When to consider an S-corporation election

Do not elect S-corporation status only because revenue begins.

An S-corporation adds:

- Payroll
- Reasonable-compensation requirements
- Separate tax filings
- Bookkeeping and compliance costs
- More administrative work

Revisit the election with a CPA when consistent annual net profit is high enough that the potential payroll-tax savings exceed the additional costs and complexity.

## A clean personal routine

### Every payment

- Save the receipt.
- Categorize the revenue.
- Move the tax reserve.
- Record the service period.
- Record crypto fair market value when applicable.

### Weekly

- Reconcile Stripe, bank and crypto receipts.
- Save invoices and expenses.
- Review failed subscriptions.

### Monthly

- Reconcile the business bank account.
- Review revenue by package.
- Confirm active features match paid terms.
- Export Stripe and wallet records.
- Review the automated business-monitor report.

### Quarterly

- Review profit with a bookkeeper or CPA.
- Pay estimated taxes or adjust W-2 withholding.
- Review package profitability.
- Confirm sales-tax treatment has not changed.

### Annually

- Issue contractor tax forms when required.
- File federal, Pennsylvania and local returns.
- Renew required registrations and insurance.
- Review whether the business structure remains appropriate.

## Professional help

Before substantial revenue, schedule a Pennsylvania CPA consultation and provide:

- Expected package prices
- Estimated annual revenue
- Current W-2 income
- Filing status
- Expected business expenses
- Planned crypto-payment volume
- Desired legal entity
- Home and local municipality
- Whether a spouse will participate in the business

The objective is compliance and tax efficiency—not hiding income or avoiding legally owed tax.
